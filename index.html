with Modal Verbs

Context: California's State Parks & Campfire Rules
🌲

Grammar Focus

4 min
modal verb  +  be  +  past participle
ModalMeaningExample
muststrong obligation / prohibitionCampfires must not be lit near dry grass.
shouldrecommendation / adviceGarbage should be put in bins.
canpermission / possibilityDogs can be taken into many parks.
mayweaker permissionCars may be parked in official lots only.
willfuture rule / certaintyNoisy dogs will not be allowed.
💡 Negative: modal + not + be + past participle  |  e.g. must not be lit   should not be built   will not be allowed

Wrap-Up · The Formula

modal verb  +  be  +  past participle
must
Strong obligation
Fires must not be lit.
should
Advice
Bins should be used.
can
Permission
Dogs can be taken in.
may
Weaker permission
Cars may be parked here.
will
Future rule
Dogs will not be allowed.
Common mistakes to avoid: ✗ Dogs should kept…  →  ✓ should be kept
✗ must be ordering  →  ✓ must be ordered
Negative form: modal + not + be + past participle
Fires must not be lit. Dogs will not be allowed.
